# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# GENERAL INDIA TRAVEL SAFETY
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

GENERAL_EMERGENCY_CONTACTS:
- National Emergency: 112 (works pan-India on any network, even without SIM)
- Police: 100
- Ambulance: 108 (free, government ambulance service)
- Fire: 101
- Women Helpline: 1091
- Tourist Helpline: 1800-111-363 (toll free, Ministry of Tourism)
- NDRF (Disaster): 011-24363260

GENERAL_SAFETY_TIPS:
- Always carry photocopy of Aadhaar/Passport — original in hotel safe
- Dress code: Conservative clothing recommended in smaller towns and religious sites (cover shoulders and knees)
- Photography: Always ask permission before photographing people, temples, military installations (illegal)
- Photography of military areas, borders, bridges, airports is PROHIBITED
- Bargaining culture: Expected at markets, not at fixed-price stores or malls
- Street food: Eat from busy stalls (high turnover = fresher food), avoid pre-cut fruits
- Water: Only drink bottled (Bisleri, Kinley, Aquafina) or filtered water — never tap water
- Ice in drinks: Often made from tap water — avoid at street stalls, safe at established restaurants
- Cash: India is still heavily cash-based, especially in smaller towns and hill areas
- ATMs: Use bank ATMs over standalone ATMs to avoid card skimming; SBI/HDFC/ICICI safest
- Scam awareness: Be wary of unsolicited help from strangers, especially at tourist sites
- Drugs: Strictly illegal throughout India. Goa's reputation does NOT make it safer. Severe penalties.
- LGBTQ+: Legal since 2018, but conservative social attitudes in most places — discretion advised
- Travel insurance: Strongly recommended — buy before departure

TRANSPORT_SAFETY:
- Always use metered/app taxis or pre-paid taxis from official counters
- Never accept unmarked vehicles or rides from strangers
- At train stations: Porters (coolies) have fixed rates displayed — pay only fixed amount
- Keep luggage under your berth or use chain-lock (provided on overnight trains)
- Overnight buses: Keep valuables in small bag with you, not in overhead

HEALTH_GENERAL:
- Vaccinations recommended: Hepatitis A & B, Typhoid, Rabies (if trekking), Tetanus
- Rabies: Real risk from stray dogs and monkeys — don't feed or approach wildlife
- Sun protection: UV index very high — SPF 50+ sunscreen mandatory
- Mosquito protection: Dengue and malaria risk in some areas — use repellent (DEET-based), mosquito net
- Stomach issues: Delhi Belly common first week — carry ORS, Norflox (antibiotic), Imodium
- Carry: Personal medications, band-aids, antiseptic cream, ORS sachets, pain relievers
- Altitude sickness: Real risk above 3,000m — see specific destination sections

WOMEN_SAFETY_GENERAL:
- India travel is manageable but requires awareness
- Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night
- Dress modestly, especially in smaller towns, religious areas, and North India
- Trust your instincts — if uncomfortable, leave the situation
- Share location with someone you trust
- Book hotel rooms with deadbolts/chain locks
- Use app taxis (OLA/Uber) — safer than hailing random taxis
- Emergency: Women Helpline 1091, Police 100

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# GOA
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Goa
SAFETY_RATING: Generally Safe (3.5/5 for solo women at night, 4.5/5 overall)
PEAK_SEASON_ALERT: December-January — very crowded, petty theft increases, prices spike
EMERGENCY:
- Goa Police: 0832-2423366 / Tourist Police: 1800-209-0110
- Goa Medical College, Panaji: 0832-2458700
- Apollo Victor Hospital (Margao): 0832-2705000
- Nearest hospital to Baga/Calangute: Mapusa Referral Hospital (15km)
BEACH_SAFETY:
- Always swim at lifeguard-patrolled beaches — flag system: Green = swim, Yellow = caution, Red = no swimming
- Rip currents kill every year — if caught in rip current, swim parallel to shore, not against it
- Avoid isolated beaches at night (Anjuna, Arambol after midnight)
- North Goa beaches rougher and more dangerous than South Goa
- Never leave belongings unattended on beach — theft common
SPECIFIC_SCAMS:
- Taxi touts at airport: Use GoaMiles app or pre-paid counter — never random taxi drivers
- Gem/stone shops: Strangers offering 'export business' gem deals are 100% scams
- Motorbike rental damage scam: Document pre-existing damage with photos before renting
- Beach massage: Agree price before massage, or firmly decline
- Restaurant switch: Always check menu before sitting, confirm prices before ordering
- Drugs: Goa has STRICT drug enforcement despite reputation — undercover police active in tourist areas. DO NOT accept drugs from strangers.
NIGHTLIFE_SAFETY:
- Don't accept drinks from strangers (drink spiking reported)
- Keep phone charged and share location with friends
- Designated driver if renting bikes — DUI checkpoints active
- Solo women: Go to clubs with groups, use OLA/Uber to return

HEALTH_GOA:
- Mosquitoes: Dengue cases reported — use repellent, especially evening and morning
- Jellyfish: Some beaches in Oct-Nov — if stung, rinse with seawater (not freshwater), remove tentacles with credit card (not hands)
- Sunburn: Very strong sun — SPF 50+ mandatory, reapply every 2 hrs
- Seafood: Avoid shellfish at questionable establishments — stick to reputable restaurants

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# RAJASTHAN
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Rajasthan
SAFETY_RATING: Safe with standard precautions (4/5)
EMERGENCY:
- Rajasthan Police: 100 | Tourist Police Jaipur: 0141-2744000 | Jodhpur: 0291-2636999
- SMS Hospital Jaipur (government): 0141-2518501
- Mahatma Gandhi Hospital Jodhpur: 0291-2434374
HEAT_WARNING:
- April-June: Temperatures 42-48°C — EXTREME danger of heatstroke
- MANDATORY: Carry 3+ liters water, drink every 30 mins
- Wear light cotton, full-sleeved to block sun
- Avoid outdoors 12pm-4pm in summer
- Signs of heatstroke: Confusion, no sweating despite heat, very high temperature — medical emergency
DESERT_SAFETY (Jaisalmer):
- NEVER venture into Sam Sand Dunes without a licensed guide
- Stay on camel/jeep safari routes — dunes shift, easy to get lost
- Carry water even on short camel rides (1L minimum)
- Dress in light, full-coverage clothing to avoid sunburn
SPECIFIC_SCAMS:
- Gem export scams: Very common in Jaipur — strangers offering to pay you for carrying gems to another country. DO NOT engage. This is either fraud or smuggling.
- Carpet/textile scam: Taxi drivers get commission to bring tourists to specific shops. Prices in these shops 3-5x higher.
- Shop recommendation by guides: Usually commission-based — research independently
- 'Free' fort guides: Claim government authorization at fort entrance — rarely official. Check Tourist Information Center for official guides (₹200-500/hr)
- Camel safari bait-and-switch: Agree on ALL details (duration, route, overnight camping included) before payment
PHOTOGRAPHY:
- Amber Fort: Photography allowed (₹200 for camera). Never photograph army/military installations.
- Local people: Always ask before photographing women, especially in rural areas
- Mehrangarh Fort has no photo fee — included in entry

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# KERALA
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Kerala
SAFETY_RATING: Very Safe — one of India's safest states (4.5/5, 4.8/5 for solo women)
EMERGENCY:
- Kerala Police Tourist Helpline: 1800-425-4747 (toll free)
- Lakeshore Hospital Kochi: 0484-2701032
- Amrita Institute of Medical Sciences Kochi: 0484-2858165
- Kozhikode Medical College: 0495-2350216
WOMEN_SAFETY: Kerala has high female literacy and relatively progressive social attitudes. Generally considered the safest Indian state for solo women travelers. Standard precautions still apply.
MONSOON_SAFETY (June-September):
- Landslide risk in Munnar, Wayanad, hill areas — REAL danger
- Flash floods in low-lying areas and backwaters — don't underestimate
- Check IMD alerts: mausam.imd.gov.in before travelling to hills during monsoon
- Wayanad 2024 landslide killed 400+ people — never ignore warnings
- Houseboat safety: Life jackets mandatory, insist on them. Check Coast Guard certification of vessel.
WATER_ACTIVITIES:
- Houseboat: Never hire uncertified operator — Kerala Tourism's certified list at keralatourism.org
- Alleppey backwaters: Don't swim in canals (pollution, currents)
- Beach: Arabian Sea currents can be strong — respect lifeguard flags
SCAMS:
- Very few major scams in Kerala compared to other tourist states
- Overcharging autorickshaws: Use Ola/Uber or agree on price before
- Gem/spice shop commission: Taxi drivers may take commission — compare prices before buying

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# HIMACHAL PRADESH (MANALI)
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Manali and Himachal Pradesh
SAFETY_RATING: Generally Safe, specific activity risks (3.8/5)
EMERGENCY:
- Manali Police: 01902-252340
- Mission Hospital Manali: 01902-252379
- Regional Hospital Kullu: 01902-222341
- Mountain Rescue: Manali Police Station 01902-252340
ALTITUDE_SAFETY:
- Rohtang Pass (3,978m): Altitude sickness possible for plains people — headache normal. Take Diamox if prescribed.
- Spiti Valley (4,000-4,500m): Acclimatize 1-2 days before going higher
- Symptoms of AMS: Persistent headache, nausea, vomiting, difficulty breathing, confusion → Descend IMMEDIATELY
- NEVER ascend if you have AMS symptoms — it can be fatal
ROAD_SAFETY:
- Mountain roads are DANGEROUS — especially Rohtang Pass and Atal Tunnel route
- Only drive in experienced vehicles — hire local taxis familiar with mountain roads
- Never drive on mountain roads after dark unless absolutely necessary
- Debris/rockfall can close roads suddenly — check HRTC and MANALI TRAFFIC alerts
- Rohtang Pass opens May/June — check permit availability online
- Kullu-Manali Highway: Regular landslides in July-August monsoon. Check conditions at NHAI.
RIVER_ACTIVITIES:
- White water rafting: Only use Grade-certified operators listed at District Tourism Office
- ALWAYS wear life jacket, helmet — non-negotiable
- Beas River water is COLD — hypothermia risk in winter/early summer
- Don't raft during heavy monsoon (July-August) — rapids can become Class 5-6
PARAGLIDING:
- Solang Valley: Licensed operators only — check Himachal Tourism certification
- Never fly in cloudy or rainy conditions
- Check weather forecast from the instructor themselves (they're in the sky too)
DRUGS: Cannabis grows wild in Parvati Valley (Kasol/Kheerganga) — legally it is ILLEGAL in India. Police raids happen. Don't carry.

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# LADAKH
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Ladakh
SAFETY_RATING: Safe, requires serious preparation (3.5/5 — the risks are all altitude/environment)
EMERGENCY:
- Leh Police: 01982-252018
- SNM Hospital Leh (ONLY major hospital): 01982-252360
- Army Referral Hospital Leh: 01982-252012 (emergency only, civilians in extreme cases)
- Helicopter evacuation: Arranged through SNM Hospital or army for serious AMS cases
ALTITUDE_SICKNESS — CRITICAL:
- Leh is at 3,524m — arrival by air is very high risk for plains people
- MANDATORY: Rest completely for 24-48 hours on arrival. No sightseeing on day 1.
- DO NOT run, climb stairs, or exert physically on day 1
- Diamox (Acetazolamide) 125mg twice daily starting 1 day before arrival — consult doctor
- Hydrate constantly — 3-4 liters water/day
- AMS symptoms: Headache (most common), nausea, fatigue, dizziness, loss of appetite
- HACE (High Altitude Cerebral Edema): Confusion, ataxia (can't walk straight), drowsiness — IMMEDIATE descent + evacuation
- HAPE (High Altitude Pulmonary Edema): Breathlessness at rest, pink frothy sputum — LIFE-THREATENING, immediate evacuation
- NEVER go higher if you have symptoms — DESCEND is the only cure
- Most hotels in Leh provide oxygen cylinders — use if severe headache
EXTREME_WEATHER:
- Temperature: Leh can drop to -20°C in winter (December-February)
- Wind at Pangong, Nubra: Very strong — layer clothing even in summer
- UV radiation: Extreme at high altitude — SPF 50+ every hour, UV-blocking sunglasses mandatory
- Pack thermals even for summer (nights at Pangong = 0-5°C in July-August)
PERMITS (Mandatory — no entry without):
- Inner Line Permit (ILP): Required for Pangong Tso, Nubra Valley, Tso Moriri, Dha-Hanu sector
- Apply online: lahdc.nic.in or at DC Office Leh (Old Power House Road) — ₹200-400 per area
- PAP (Protected Area Permit): Foreign nationals need additional permit for same areas
- Nathula (Sikkim border): Separate permit — NOT included in Ladakh ILP
- Keep permit copies — checkpoints at entry to each restricted area
CONNECTIVITY:
- Only BSNL postpaid works beyond Leh city (Pangong, Nubra, Tso Moriri have no signal of other networks)
- Buy BSNL SIM at Leh Airport or Market — keep it active before going to remote areas
- Download offline maps on Maps.me before leaving Leh
MILITARY_AREAS: Ladakh is a strategic military zone — do NOT photograph any military vehicles, soldiers, installations, or border infrastructure. Confiscation of devices and detention possible.

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ANDAMAN ISLANDS
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Andaman Islands
SAFETY_RATING: Very Safe (4.5/5)
EMERGENCY:
- Andaman Police: 03192-233107
- GB Pant Hospital Port Blair: 03192-232102
- Coast Guard: 03192-234144 (for marine emergencies)
DIVING_SAFETY:
- Only dive with PADI/SSI certified dive operators — many are not certified, verify
- Never dive alone — always buddy system
- Decompression sickness is real — nearest hyperbaric chamber at GB Pant Hospital Port Blair
- Don't touch coral — it takes 100 years to grow 1 inch. Heavy fines for coral damage.
TRIBAL_PROTECTION:
- Sentinelese (North Sentinel Island): ALL contact strictly PROHIBITED by Indian law
- Jarawas (Middle Andaman): Do not attempt to interact or photograph from vehicles — ₹1 lakh fine
- Onge, Shompen: Protected — no contact permitted
FERRY_SAFETY:
- Government ferries are older — less comfortable but operational. Check lifejacket availability before boarding.
- Makruzz and Nautika: Private, well-maintained, air-conditioned — preferred
- Sea can be rough November-January — seasickness tablets (Avomine) recommended
- During monsoon (June-September): Many services suspended

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# PRE-TRIP SAFETY CHECKLIST (ALL DESTINATIONS)
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PRE_DEPARTURE_CHECKLIST:
- Travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage (especially for Ladakh and adventure activities)
- Photocopies of all documents — stored separately from originals
- Share detailed itinerary with family/friend at home with check-in schedule
- Emergency contacts saved offline on phone
- Download offline maps for all destinations before leaving connectivity
- Local SIM card plan — buy at airport arrival
- First aid kit: ORS, Norflox, Imodium, Bandages, Antiseptic, Paracetamol, personal meds
- Altitude kit (if Ladakh/Manali): Diamox (prescription), pulse oximeter (very useful, ₹800-1,500)
- Cash: India is cash-heavy — carry sufficient local currency
- Bank alert: Notify bank about travel dates to avoid card being blocked
