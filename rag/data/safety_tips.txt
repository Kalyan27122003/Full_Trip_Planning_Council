DESTINATION: Goa
SAFETY_LEVEL: Generally Safe
TIPS:
- Avoid isolated beaches at night, stick to popular areas
- Keep valuables in hotel safe, avoid displaying expensive jewelry
- Be cautious of tourist scams at markets, negotiate prices before buying
- Water safety: Only swim at lifeguard-patrolled beaches (flag system: green=safe, red=danger)
- Nightlife safety: Don't accept drinks from strangers
- Drug laws are strictly enforced despite Goa's reputation
EMERGENCY:
- Police: 100 | Tourist Helpline: 1800-209-0110
- Hospitals: Goa Medical College (Panaji), Apollo Victor Hospital
- Women safety: Relatively safe but take standard precautions

DESTINATION: Rajasthan
SAFETY_LEVEL: Safe with precautions
TIPS:
- Heat advisory: Summer (April-June) temperatures exceed 45°C, carry water always
- Camel/horse riding: Use licensed operators only, avoid street touts
- Bargaining scams: Fixed-price shops recommended for first purchases
- Desert safety: Never go on camel safari without guide
- Photography: Always ask permission before photographing locals/religious sites
- Gem/textile scams: Very common, avoid commission shops recommended by taxi drivers
EMERGENCY:
- Police: 100 | Tourist Police: Jaipur (0141-2744000)
- Hospitals: SMS Hospital Jaipur, Umaid Hospital Jodhpur

DESTINATION: Kerala
SAFETY_LEVEL: Very Safe
TIPS:
- Monsoon travel (June-August): Landslide risk in hill areas, check weather
- Water activities: Use life jackets on houseboats, check operator certifications
- Wildlife sanctuaries: Follow forest department guidelines strictly
- Street food: Generally safe but stick to cooked hot food
- Heat and humidity: Stay hydrated, wear light cotton clothes
EMERGENCY:
- Police: 100 | Tourist Helpline: 1800-425-4747
- Hospitals: Lakeshore Hospital Kochi, Amrita Institute Kochi
- Women safety: One of the safest states for women travelers

DESTINATION: Manali
SAFETY_LEVEL: Mostly Safe, Adventure risks
TIPS:
- Mountain sickness: Acclimatize for 1-2 days before high altitude activities
- Road safety: Rohtang Pass can be dangerous in bad weather, check conditions
- River activities: Only use certified operators for rafting, check safety equipment
- Winter travel: Roads can close without notice, carry extra warm clothes
- Paragliding: Use licensed operators certified by Himachal Tourism
- Trekking: Never trek alone, inform hotel of trek plans
EMERGENCY:
- Police: 100 | Mountain Rescue: Manali Police (01902-252340)
- Hospitals: Mission Hospital Manali, Regional Hospital Kullu
- Altitude sickness: Descend immediately if symptoms appear

DESTINATION: Ladakh
SAFETY_LEVEL: Requires preparation
TIPS:
- Altitude sickness (AMS): Very serious risk, acclimatize 2-3 days in Leh
- Symptoms to watch: Headache, nausea, dizziness — descend if severe
- Oxygen availability: Many hotels provide oxygen cylinders
- Road safety: Mountain roads are extreme, hire experienced local drivers
- Temperature: Can drop to -20°C in winter, pack thermal layers
- Inner Line Permits: Required for restricted areas, get in advance
- Mobile connectivity: Only BSNL/Airtel work in most areas
- ATMs: Very limited, carry sufficient cash from Leh
EMERGENCY:
- Police: 100 | Army helpline available for serious emergencies
- Hospitals: SNM Hospital Leh (only well-equipped hospital)
- Evacuation: Air evacuation for serious cases, travel insurance mandatory

GENERAL_TRAVEL_SAFETY_INDIA:
- Always carry a photocopy of passport/ID
- Use only prepaid taxis from official counters at airports/stations
- Keep emergency contacts saved offline
- Travel insurance recommended for all trips
- Share your itinerary with someone back home
- Keep ₹500-1000 emergency cash separate from main wallet
- Download offline maps (Maps.me or Google Maps offline)
- Emergency number: 112 (works pan-India)
