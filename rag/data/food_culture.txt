DESTINATION: Goa
MUST_EAT:
- Fish Curry Rice: Staple Goan meal, best at beach shacks
- Prawn Balchão: Spicy pickled prawn curry
- Bebinca: Traditional layered Goan dessert
- Vindaloo: Fiery pork/chicken curry
- Feni: Local cashew/coconut liquor (must try!)
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Fisherman's Wharf (South Goa): Authentic seafood, riverside dining
- Britto's (Baga Beach): Famous landmark, party vibes
- Gunpowder (Assagao): South Indian meets Goan fusion
- Vinayak Family Restaurant (Margao): Local authentic thali
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Carnival of Goa (February): Colorful street parade
- Flea Markets: Anjuna (Wednesday), Ingo's Saturday Night Market
- Old Goa Churches: UNESCO Heritage, St. Francis of Assisi
- Spice Plantation Tour: Interactive cooking + lunch

DESTINATION: Rajasthan
MUST_EAT:
- Dal Baati Churma: Iconic Rajasthani dish, ghee-loaded
- Laal Maas: Fiery mutton curry
- Ghewar: Disc-shaped sweet, especially during festivals
- Mirchi Bada: Spicy stuffed chilli fritters
- Pyaaz Kachori: Flaky onion-stuffed pastry
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Chokhi Dhani (Jaipur): Village theme, authentic Rajasthani thali
- Spice Court (Jaipur): Award-winning local cuisine
- Mihir Garh (Jodhpur): Luxury desert dining
- Trio Restaurant (Jaisalmer): Rooftop with fort view
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Pushkar Camel Fair (November): World's largest camel fair
- Jaipur Literature Festival (January): World's biggest free literary festival
- Puppet shows and folk dances: Daily at cultural centers
- Block printing workshops: Sanganer and Bagru villages

DESTINATION: Kerala
MUST_EAT:
- Sadya: Grand vegetarian feast on banana leaf (Sunday tradition)
- Appam with Stew: Lacy rice pancakes with coconut milk stew
- Karimeen Pollichathu: Pearl spot fish wrapped in banana leaf
- Kerala Prawn Curry: Coconut milk based, served with rice
- Puttu and Kadala Curry: Steamed rice cake with black chickpea curry
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Dhe Puttu (Kochi): Famous for creative puttu variations
- Paragon Restaurant (Kozhikode): Legendary Malabar biryani
- Malabar Junction (Kovalam): Fine dining with sea view
- Backwater Ripples (Alleppey): Authentic Kerala meals
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Kathakali Dance: Classical dance-drama, colorful costumes
- Kalaripayattu: Ancient martial art performance
- Onam Festival (August-September): Grand harvest celebration
- Thrissur Pooram: Spectacular temple festival with elephants

DESTINATION: Manali
MUST_EAT:
- Siddu: Local stuffed bread with ghee
- Trout Fish: Fresh from Beas river, grilled or fried
- Dham: Traditional Himachali festive meal on leaf plates
- Chha Gosht: Marinated lamb in yogurt gravy
- Aktori: Buckwheat pancake, local breakfast
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Johnson's Cafe (Manali): Pioneer of Manali dining, trout speciality
- Drifter's Inn & Cafe (Old Manali): Hippie vibes, international food
- Lazy Dog Lounge (Old Manali): Great views, multi-cuisine
- Chopsticks (Mall Road): Chinese and Tibetan food
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Hadimba Temple: Unique pagoda-style temple in cedar forest
- Tibetan Monastery: Gadhan Thekchhokling Gompa in Old Manali
- Naggar Castle: 500-year-old heritage castle
- Kullu Dussehra Festival (October): International fair, unique celebrations

DESTINATION: Ladakh
MUST_EAT:
- Thukpa: Tibetan noodle soup, perfect for cold weather
- Momos: Steamed/fried dumplings with meat or veg filling
- Tsampa: Roasted barley flour, staple Ladakhi food
- Butter Tea (Gur Gur Chai): Salted yak butter tea
- Skyu: Thick stew with hand-rolled pasta and root vegetables
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Bon Appetit (Leh): Best momos and Ladakhi cuisine
- Tibetan Kitchen (Leh): Authentic Tibetan food
- Nezer View Restaurant (Leh): Rooftop with great mountain views
- The Open Hand (Leh): Organic cafe, great breakfast
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Hemis Festival (June-July): Largest monastery festival, masked dances
- Losar (Ladakhi New Year, December-January): Traditional celebrations
- Archery Festival: Traditional sport competitions
- Stok Palace Museum: Royal artifacts and costumes
