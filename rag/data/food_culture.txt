# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# GOA
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Goa
CUISINE_TYPE: Goan (Portuguese-influenced, heavy on seafood, coconut milk, Kokum)
SIGNATURE_DISHES:
- Fish Curry Rice (Xitti Kodi): Staple Goan meal, tangy coconut-tamarind curry with kingfish or pomfret — order at any local restaurant (₹180-350)
- Prawn Balchão: Fiery pickled prawn curry in vinegar and chili — classic Goan flavour bomb (₹300-500)
- Chicken/Pork Cafreal: Portuguese-influenced green herb marinade, grilled (₹250-400)
- Pork Vindaloo: Famous fiery pork curry in vinegar-chili base, originally Portuguese Vinha d'Alhos (₹280-450)
- Goan Sausage (Chouriço): Spiced pork sausage, sold in links — try sausage pulao or pan-fry (₹200-350)
- Bebinca: Traditional 7-layer Goan dessert made with coconut milk, egg yolk, ghee — must have (₹150-250/slice)
- Dodol: Dark, sticky toffee-like sweet made from coconut milk and jaggery (₹80-150)
- Xacuti (Shaguti): Coconut-roasted spice curry with chicken or mutton, complex flavour (₹280-450)
STREET_FOOD:
- Ros Omelette: Egg omelette soaked in Goan curry sauce, served in bread roll — iconic street food ₹30-50 (Mapusa Market)
- Pav Bhaji at Baga Beach stalls: Local version with Goan touch (₹80-120)
- Fresh coconut water: ₹30-50 from vendors on every beach
- Boiled corn: Beach staple ₹30-50
MUST_DRINK:
- Feni: Local Goa spirit — Cashew feni (from cashew apple) and Coconut feni (from toddy palm). Try at any bar or local tavern. ₹150-400/glass
- King's Beer: Local Goa beer, very popular (₹90-150/bottle)
- Fresh sugarcane juice: ₹30-50
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Fisherman's Wharf (South Goa, Cavelossim): Riverside location, exceptional seafood, live music weekends — ₹600-1,200/person
- Vinayak Family Restaurant (Assagao): Authentic Goan home cooking, families love it, no frills, great fish curry — ₹300-500/person
- Gunpowder (Assagao): Kerala-Goa fusion, rooftop, famous for avial and prawn preparations — ₹700-1,200/person
- Britto's (Baga Beach): Landmark restaurant, buzzing atmosphere, seafood platter — ₹500-900/person
- Souza Lobo (Calangute): Est. 1932, one of Goa's oldest restaurants, classic Goan fare — ₹500-800/person
- Martin's Corner (Betalbatim, South Goa): Famous for crab, recommended by food critics — ₹600-1,000/person
- Thalassa (Vagator): Greek-Mediterranean, cliff views, excellent cocktails, romantic — ₹800-1,500/person
- The Black Sheep Bistro (Panaji): Fine dining, craft cocktails, best for special dinners — ₹1,200-2,000/person
STREET_FOOD_AREAS:
- Mapusa Friday Market: Goa's biggest local market, all fresh produce, local food stalls
- Anjuna Wednesday Flea Market: Tourist market with food stalls from across India
- Panaji Municipal Market: Real local experience, fresh fish market, local snacks
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Goa Carnival (February-March): 4-day pre-Lent festival, Portuguese legacy, street parades, music, floats
- Shigmo Festival (March): Hindu spring festival, colorful processions, folk dances
- Christmas in Goa (December): Magical atmosphere in Old Goa churches, midnight mass, illuminations
- Feast of St. Francis Xavier (December 3): 10-yearly exposition of body relics, massive pilgrimage
- Old Goa Church Walking Tour: Basilica of Bom Jesus, Se Cathedral, Church of St. Francis of Assisi — UNESCO heritage
- Spice Plantation Tour (Ponda): Sahakari Spice Farm, Savoi — guided tour with lunch on banana leaf ₹500-800
- Goa State Museum: Pre-Portuguese and Portuguese era artifacts, best museum in state
- Goa Heritage Walk (Panaji): Fontainhas Latin Quarter, Portuguese-era houses, azulejo tiles
CULTURAL_ETIQUETTE:
- Remove shoes before entering temples and churches
- Dress modestly at religious sites (cover shoulders and knees)
- Topless sunbathing technically illegal but tolerated at some beaches
- Don't bargain aggressively at markets — gentle negotiation is fine
FOOD_MARKET: Mapusa on Fridays, Anjuna on Wednesdays, Saturday Night Market (Arpora) for crafts and food

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# RAJASTHAN
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Rajasthan (Jaipur / Jodhpur / Jaisalmer / Udaipur)
CUISINE_TYPE: Rajasthani (Marwari) — rich, ghee-laden, vegetarian-dominant, spicy, preserved foods (historically adapted to desert conditions)
SIGNATURE_DISHES:
- Dal Baati Churma: King of Rajasthani food — baked wheat balls (baati) dipped in ghee, served with 5-dal mix and sweetened churma — must eat ₹150-300
- Laal Maas: Fiery red mutton curry with Mathania chilies (Jodhpuri specialty) — not for the faint-hearted ₹350-600
- Gatte ki Sabzi: Chickpea flour dumplings in spiced yogurt gravy, common household dish ₹150-250
- Ker Sangri: Desert beans and berries, preserved vegetable unique to Rajasthan ₹180-300
- Pyaaz Kachori: Flaky pastry filled with spiced onion, Jaipur street staple ₹30-60 from Rawat Mishthan Bhandar
- Mirchi Bada: Giant chili fritter filled with spiced potato — ₹20-40
- Mawa Kachori: Sweet kachori filled with khoya and dry fruits, Jodhpur speciality ₹40-80
- Ghewar: Disc-shaped honeycomb sweet soaked in sugar syrup and cream, especially during Teej and Gangaur festivals
- Malpua: Sweet pancake in sugar syrup, popular at festive time
STREET_FOOD_AREAS:
- Jaipur: MI Road, Johri Bazaar, Bapu Bazaar food stalls — best for kachori, jalebi, chai
- Jodhpur: Clock Tower area (Ghanta Ghar) — best street food hub in Rajasthan
- Jaisalmer: Near Jaisalmer Fort — local dhabas with bajra roti and laal maas
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Chokhi Dhani (Jaipur, Tonk Road): Ethnic village theme resort, traditional Rajasthani thali served on floor with folk performances — ₹700-900/person including entry
- 1135 AD (Amber Fort, Jaipur): Inside Amber Fort, royal Rajasthani cuisine — ₹900-1,800/person, magnificent setting
- Laxmi Misthan Bhandar — LMB (Johari Bazaar, Jaipur): Est. 1727, famous for sweets and Rajasthani thali — ₹400-700/person
- Trio Restaurant (Jaisalmer): Rooftop with fort view, decent food, great ambiance — ₹400-700/person
- On the Rocks (Jodhpur): Garden restaurant near Umaid Bhawan, excellent laal maas — ₹600-1,000/person
- Mihir Garh Dining (Jodhpur): Luxury desert resort dining, elaborate Rajasthani meals — ₹1,500-2,500/person
- Ambrai Restaurant (Udaipur): Floating restaurant on Lake Pichola, Lake Palace views — ₹800-1,400/person
- Natraj Dining Hall (Udaipur): Unlimited Rajasthani thali, very popular, queue expected — ₹250-400/person
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Pushkar Camel Fair (November): One of world's largest camel fairs, 200,000 animals, cultural spectacle
- Jaipur Literature Festival (January): World's largest free literary festival, attracts global authors
- Desert Festival Jaisalmer (February): Camel races, turban-tying contest, folk music, Mr. Desert competition
- Gangaur Festival (March-April): Women-centric festival celebrating Goddess Gauri, colorful processions
- Teej Festival (July-August): Swing festival celebrating monsoon and Parvati, women's celebration
- Kalbelia Dance: UNESCO heritage Rajasthani folk dance by Kalbelia tribe — snake charmers' dance
- Ghoomar Dance: Traditional women's folk dance, colorful swirling skirts — seen at cultural shows
- Puppet Shows (Kathputli): Traditional Rajasthani marionette show, evening shows at hotels and cultural centers
- Block Printing Workshops: Sanganer (Jaipur, 16km) and Bagru (30km) villages — learn traditional craft
- Blue Pottery Workshop: Jaipur, UNESCO heritage craft — try making a piece yourself
- Lac Bangle Making: Jaipur old city, traditional jewelry craft experience
CULTURAL_ETIQUETTE:
- Photography inside temples and at religious sites requires permission
- Remove footwear at all temples and mosques
- Dress conservatively, especially at religious sites
- Bargaining expected and enjoyed at bazaars — start at 40-50% of asking price

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# KERALA
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Kerala
CUISINE_TYPE: Keralite — coconut-based, rice-centric, seafood-heavy, distinct from rest of South India
SIGNATURE_DISHES:
- Sadya: Grand vegetarian feast served on banana leaf — 24-28 items including avial, thoran, sambar, rasam, payasam — eaten with right hand only. Best on Onam (₹200-500, or complimentary at temples during Onam)
- Appam with Stew: Lacy fermented rice pancake with coconut milk-based vegetable/chicken stew — perfect breakfast ₹80-150
- Puttu and Kadala Curry: Steamed cylindrical rice cake with black chickpea curry — classic Kerala breakfast ₹60-120
- Kerala Fish Curry: Pearl spot (Karimeen) or sardines in tangy red coconut curry — must eat ₹250-450
- Karimeen Pollichathu: Pearl spot fish marinated in spices, wrapped in banana leaf and pan fried — ₹350-600
- Malabar Biryani: Kerala's version of biryani using Khyma rice — lighter, aromatic (Kozhikode specialty) ₹250-400
- Thalassery Biryani: Distinct style from northern Kerala, with fried onions and cashews ₹250-400
- Erissery: Pumpkin and red bean curry in coconut — festival dish, earthy and comforting ₹120-200
- Olan: Ash gourd and red cowpeas in thin coconut milk — subtle, delicate sadya dish
- Prawn Moilee: Prawns in light coconut milk and turmeric gravy — very gentle flavour ₹300-500
- Kerala Beef Fry: Dry-cooked tender beef with shallots and coconut — extremely popular ₹200-350
- Unniyappam: Small deep-fried rice balls with jaggery and banana — temple prasad and snack ₹30-60 for 5 pieces
BREAKFAST_MUST_TRY:
- Idiyappam (String Hoppers) with coconut milk: Delicate steamed rice noodles ₹60-100
- Porotta with Beef Curry: Layered flatbread with Kerala beef curry — cult favorite ₹100-180
- Dosa varieties: Kerala-style (thinner, crispier) with sambar and 3 chutneys ₹60-120
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Paragon Restaurant (Calicut/Kozhikode): Legendary since 1939, best Malabar biryani in Kerala — ₹250-500/person, always crowded
- Dhe Puttu (Kochi, multiple locations): Famous for creative puttu variations, long queues — ₹150-300/person
- Malabar Junction (Fort Kochi): Rooftop fine dining, contemporary Kerala cuisine, colonial setting — ₹800-1,500/person
- Kashi Art Cafe (Fort Kochi): Breakfast hotspot, Western + Kerala fusion, artist haunt — ₹200-400/person
- Backwater Ripples Houseboat: Authentic Kerala meals served on houseboat, 4-course meal included — included in houseboat price
- Saravanaa Bhavan (Kochi, multiple): Best South Indian vegetarian chain, reliable quality — ₹150-300/person
- Fort House Restaurant (Fort Kochi): Waterfront dining, fresh catch, colonial hotel setting — ₹600-1,000/person
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Kathakali Performance: Classical dance-drama, elaborate makeup and costumes — daily shows at Kerala Kathakali Centre, Fort Kochi (₹350-500). Arrive early to see makeup process.
- Kalaripayattu Show: Ancient martial art, fluid and dynamic — daily shows at CVN Kalari and ENS Kalari, Kochi (₹200-400)
- Onam Festival (August-September): Kerala's biggest festival, boat races, Pookalam flower rangoli, grand Sadya feast, cultural programs — 10-day celebration
- Thrissur Pooram (April-May): World's most spectacular temple festival, decorated elephants, percussion orchestras, fireworks — UNESCO cultural heritage
- Nehru Trophy Boat Race (August): Traditional snake boat race on Punnamada Lake, Alleppey — 100+ boats, 150,000 spectators
- Theyyam Ritual (November-March): Unique North Kerala ritual dance, possession ceremony — raw, powerful cultural experience (mostly in Kannur district)
- Spice Market Tour (Kochi): Mattancherry spice market, centuries-old trading hub — free to walk, fascinating
- Jewish Synagogue & Paradesi Jews Heritage (Kochi): 16th century synagogue, Jew Town antique shops
- Chinese Fishing Nets (Fort Kochi): Cantilever fishing nets introduced by Chinese traders, 600 years old — photogenic at sunset
FOOD_ETIQUETTE:
- Sadya must be eaten with right hand only (left hand considered unclean)
- Banana leaf position matters — top folded = ate well, bottom fold = dissatisfied
- Rice served last as it's considered the main part of the meal
- Wash hands in the water bowl provided before and after eating

# HIMACHAL PRADESH (MANALI / DHARAMSALA)
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Himachal Pradesh (Manali / Kullu / Dharamsala)
CUISINE_TYPE: Himachali — wholesome, rustic, valley-specific ingredients, Tibetan influence in higher areas
SIGNATURE_DISHES:
- Siddu: Steamed bun filled with poppy seeds/walnuts/peas, dipped in ghee or mutton curry — quintessential Manali food ₹60-100
- Trout Fish: Fresh from Beas/Kullu rivers — grilled, tandoori or masala — ₹350-600
- Dham: Traditional Himachali festive meal served on leaf plates — rajma, kaddu (pumpkin), mad (black lentils), meetha bhaat (sweet rice) — at community events and local restaurants ₹150-250
- Chha Gosht: Marinated lamb in yogurt and gram flour gravy — elaborate preparation ₹350-600
- Aktori: Buckwheat pancake with local herbs, traditional breakfast in Lahaul-Spiti ₹60-100
- Babru: Local version of kachori stuffed with black gram — Shimla specialty ₹30-50
- Patande: Himachali-style pancakes made from wheat flour, eaten with honey or ghee ₹60-100
TIBETAN_INFLUENCE (Dharamsala / McLeod Ganj area):
- Momos: Tibetan dumplings — steamed or fried, veg/chicken/pork — everywhere ₹80-150/plate
- Thukpa: Hearty noodle soup with vegetables and meat — warming in cold climate ₹100-200
- Butter Tea (Po Cha): Salt and yak butter tea — acquired taste ₹30-60
- Thenthuk: Hand-pulled noodle soup ₹100-180
- Tingmo: Steamed Tibetan bread ₹30-60
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Johnson's Cafe (Manali): Est. 1990s, pioneer of Manali dining, legendary trout preparations — ₹400-800/person
- Drifter's Inn & Cafe (Old Manali): Hippie vibes, excellent Israeli food, multi-cuisine — ₹250-500/person
- Lazy Dog Lounge (Old Manali): Great views, pancakes, pasta, chill music — ₹300-600/person
- Chopsticks (Mall Road, Manali): Popular Chinese and Tibetan food — ₹200-400/person
- Nick's Italian Kitchen (McLeod Ganj): Best pizza in the mountains — ₹300-500/person
- Illiterati (McLeod Ganj): Books + coffee + food, traveler favorite — ₹200-400/person
- Woeser Tibetan Kitchen (McLeod Ganj): Authentic Tibetan home cooking — ₹150-300/person
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Hadimba Devi Temple: Unique 1553 CE pagoda-style temple in cedar forest, important local deity
- Naggar Castle: 500-year-old castle with Nicholas Roerich Art Gallery — Russian artist who loved Kullu valley
- Tibetan Institute of Performing Arts (McLeod Ganj): Tibetan opera and dance performances
- Tsuglagkhang Complex (McLeod Ganj): Dalai Lama's temple, Tibet Museum, Buddhist library
- Kullu Dussehra (October): International fair, unique as it celebrates end of Dussehra — 7 days, 300+ local deities come to pay respect
- Manali Winter Carnival (January): Snow sculpture, ski competition, folk performances
- Local Kullu Shawl Weaving: Visit weaving workshops, buy authentic handloom shawls (₹800-5,000)
- Tibetan Market (Dharamsala/Manali): Tibetan handicrafts, thangka paintings, singing bowls, prayer flags

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# LADAKH
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Ladakh
CUISINE_TYPE: Ladakhi-Tibetan — hardy, warming, adapted for high altitude
SIGNATURE_DISHES:
- Thukpa: Tibetan noodle soup with meat/vegetables — primary meal in Ladakh, warming at high altitude ₹120-200
- Momos: Steamed/fried dumplings — veg or meat — mandatory Ladakh experience ₹100-180/plate
- Skyu: Thick stew with hand-rolled pasta pieces and root vegetables — traditional winter food ₹120-200
- Tsampa: Roasted barley flour, mixed with butter tea to make dough — staple energy food for trekkers
- Butter Tea (Gur Gur Chai): Salted yak butter tea, churned in bamboo cylinder — essential warmth ₹30-60
- Chang: Local barley beer, slightly fermented, cloudy appearance — try at local homes (offered as hospitality)
- Chhang: Slightly different preparation — most households make their own
- Paba: Thick barley paste eaten with leafy greens — traditional Ladakhi meal
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Bon Appetit (Leh main bazaar): Best momos in Leh, also great Ladakhi thalis — ₹150-300/person
- Tibetan Kitchen (Fort Road, Leh): Authentic Tibetan food, prayer wheel outside — ₹150-300/person
- Nezer View Restaurant (Leh): Rooftop with mountain views, multicuisine — ₹200-400/person
- The Open Hand (Changspa, Leh): Organic café, excellent breakfast, traveler hangout — ₹200-350/person
- Alchi Kitchen (Alchi Village): Traditional Ladakhi food in a 1,000-year-old village — ₹200-350/person
- La Piazzetta (Leh): Italian in Ladakh — great pasta and wood-fired pizza (surprisingly good) ₹300-500/person
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Hemis Festival (June-July): Ladakh's biggest monastery festival — Cham masked dances, thangka unveiling (world's largest thangka, 90ft tall, displayed once in 12 years)
- Losar (Ladakhi New Year, Dec-Jan): 15-day celebration, traditional dress, archery, folk dances
- Ladakh Festival (September): Government-organized, showcases all Ladakhi arts — polo, archery, folk dance, monastery music
- Sindhu Darshan Festival (June): Festival celebrating Indus river, held at Shey
- Monastery Music at Thiksey: Morning prayers with trumpets and drums — ethereal experience (6am, free entry)
- Archery Competition: Traditional sport, competitions held at Leh's main ground
- Stok Palace Museum: Royal family artifacts, costumes, ancient thangkas — ₹100 entry
- Village Homestay Program: Stay with Ladakhi families, learn traditional lifestyle, farm activities — ₹1,500-2,500/night including meals

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# ANDAMAN ISLANDS
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESTINATION: Andaman Islands
CUISINE_TYPE: Andamanese — seafood-dominant, Bengali and South Indian influence, fresh tropical ingredients
SIGNATURE_DISHES:
- Grilled Fish (fresh catch): Red snapper, parrot fish, barracuda — grilled with local spices ₹250-450
- Lobster: Port Blair restaurants, seasonal (₹800-2,000 depending on size and preparation)
- Crab Masala: Blue swimmer or mud crab in spiced curry ₹400-700
- Prawn curry: Bengali-style in mustard oil or Andaman-style in coconut ₹250-400
- Coconut Prawn: Andaman specialty — prawns in fresh coconut preparation ₹300-500
- Fresh Coconut Toddy: Mildly fermented coconut palm sap — local drink ₹50-100
BEST_RESTAURANTS:
- Ananda Restaurant (Port Blair, Aberdeen Bazaar): Best local seafood, excellent crab — ₹400-700/person
- Anju Coco Resto (Havelock): Beach restaurant, fresh grilled fish, sunset views — ₹350-600/person
- Something Different Restaurant (Havelock): Good for lunch, fresh catch of day — ₹300-500/person
- Full Moon Cafe (Neil Island): Beach cafe, fresh juice, grilled fish — ₹250-450/person
CULTURAL_EXPERIENCES:
- Cellular Jail Light & Sound Show: Evening show at 6pm and 7:15pm — very moving account of freedom fighters, ₹50-100 entry
- Andaman Water Sports Complex: Official water sports, glass-bottom boat rides
- Anthropological Museum (Port Blair): Indigenous Andamanese tribes exhibits — free entry
- Ross Island: Former British capital, ruins of Chief Commissioner's residence, church, baker — peacocks and deer roam free
- Jarawa Tribal Reserve Drive (Baratang): Drive through protected reserve, Jarawa tribe visible sometimes (controversial) — prior permission needed
